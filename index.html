Noel A. Heim, Ph.D.

I am primarily interested in the evolution of marine organisms, ecosystems, and environments. My work seeks to understand both the patterns and processes that have been shaping and reshaping our ocean ecosystems over the past 700 million years. This includes quantifying evolutionary trends, interrogating the evolutionary history of body size, measuring the ecological impact of extinction, and evaluating extinction threat in modern species.

TEACHING

Principles of Paleontology

EOS 38

This course surveys the key patterns in the evolution of marine ecosystems across Earth’s 4.5-billion-year history and interprets these patterns in light of theories and evidence from geology, paleontology, geochemistry, ecology, and marine biology. In this course, students will learn how to identify fossils from the major groups of marine invertebrates and interpret their modes of life; use field methods common in paleontology to collect and document fossils in the field; address research questions in paleobiology using data from the fossil record; and write R code to analyze paleobiological data using basic statistical methods.

Mass Extinctions: The Past, Present & Future of Biodiversity

EOS 15

The history of Earth's biodiversity is punctuated by intervals of geologically rapid species loss, so-called mass extinctions. Mass extinctions have had a profound impact on biodiversity trends and the structure of today's biosphere. In this seminar-style course, we will explore the historical development mass extinctions as a concept, the causes and consequences of the "Big 5" Phanerozoic mass extinctions, and attempt to answer the question "Are we in the midst of the 6th mass extinction?"

RESEARCH

Overview

At Tufts, we are interested in reconstructing the history of Earth’s marine biosphere, including ongoing and future Anthropogenic change. Our research on this broad interdisciplinary problem focuses on environmental, physiological, ecological, and evolutionary processes, and deploys a variety of methods, including analysis of large databases, such as the Paleobiology Database and Macrostrat, statistical modeling, and field work.
